**AI, Quantum Computing and Inclusive Computing Make Huge Strides in 2021**

**October 5, 2021**

**Duke ECE launches three new centers in AI and inclusive computing and joins a national collaborative to advance quantum technologies.**

**$20M from NSF Establishes Athena Center for AI**

* Athena seeks to advance next-generation mobile networks and make fast, secure edge computing accessible to more users.

**Duke Professors to Lead Nationwide Effort to Diversify Participation in Computing**

* New $10 million grant looks to increase computing entry, retention and degree completion rates of students from historically underrepresented groups.

**Duke Leads New $5M Air Force Center of Excellence**

* National initiative will develop AI-informed communication and networking protocols fast and reliable enough to handle Air Force requirements.

**Duke Engineers Join $25M Quantum Leap Challenge Institute**

* The Duke Quantum Center's devices will be the main experimental resource in the collaborative NSF program, which is led by the University of Maryland.
