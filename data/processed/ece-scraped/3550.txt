**An Overview of High Performance Computing and Future Requirements**

**Presenter:**
Jack Dongarra

**Date and Time:**
Tuesday, May 9, 2023
12:00PM - 1:00PM

**Location:**
Lunch will be served at 11:30 am.

**Abstract:**
- Examines the changes in high performance computing over the last ten years.
- Explores future trends and the impact on numerical scientific software.
- Discusses the need for a new generation of software libraries and algorithms.
- Highlights software and algorithm challenges, such as managing communication and memory hierarchies.

**Speaker Bio:**
- Jack Dongarra is an expert in numerical algorithms in linear algebra, parallel computing, and advanced computer architectures.
- Holds appointments at the University of Manchester, Oak Ridge National Laboratory, and the University of Tennessee.
- Founded the Innovative Computing Laboratory at the University of Tennessee.
- Received numerous awards, including the ACM/SIAM Computational Science and Engineering Prize (2019), IEEE-CS Computer Pioneer Award (2020), and ACM A.M. Turing Award (2021).
